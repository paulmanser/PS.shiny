Design section of the Dichotomous dialog box

Matched or Independent?

For Case-Control studies

Choose Matched or Paired if cases and controls are matched in such a way that exposure in cases may be correlated with exposure in controls.

Choose Independent if exposure in cases and controls is uncorrelated.

For Prospective Studies

Choose Matched or Paired if patients receiving the experimental or control treatments are matched or paired in such a way that the outcome in experimental subjects may be correlated with the outcome in matched controls. In these studies the alternative hypothesis must be expressed as an odds ratio.

Choose Independent if the outcomes of cases and controls are uncorrelated.

N.B. In a case-control study we determine past exposure in cases who have disease and controls who do not. In prospective studies we determine outcomes in experimental subjects who are exposed and control subjects who are not.
