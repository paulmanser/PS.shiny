Output section of the Survival dialog box

The Output section is where the user selects the result to be generated by the PS program. In the Output field select one of the following (to make a selection, click in the Output box then click the desired output).

Sample size

Number of experimental patients n that must be recruited to detect a true hazard ratio (relative risk) R with a specified power, given m controls per experimental subject, Type I error probability α, and median survival time m1 on the control treatment. The alternative hypothesis may also be specified in terms of m1 and m2, the median survival time on the experimental treatment.

Power

The probability of correctly rejecting the null hypothesis of equal treatment survival times given n experimental patients, m control patients per experimental patient, Type I error probability α, and true median survival time m1 and hazard ratio R (or true median survival times m1 and m2).

Detectable alternative

The true hazard ratios (or median survival times on the experimental treatment) that can be detected with a specified power given n experimental patients, m controls per experimental patient, Type I error probability α, and a median survival time m1 on the control treatment.
