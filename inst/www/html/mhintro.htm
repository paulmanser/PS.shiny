Mantel-Haenszel Test Overview

Suppose that study subjects are either cases or controls and are either exposed or not exposed to some risk factor. These subjects are stratified by some confounding variable into T distinct strata. For each statum we have a separate 2x2 contingency table for cases and controls who are or are not exposed. We postulate that the within stratum odds-ratio for disease in exposed versus unexposed subjects is constant across strata. This is the odds-ratio for disease associated with exposure that is adjusted for the confounding variable that defines the strata. The null hypothesis of the Mantel-Haenszel test is that this adjusted odds ratio equals 1. We use Wittes and Wallenstein's (1987) method for sample size and power calculations for this test.

Each null hypothesis is tested with respect to a two-sided alternative hypothesis.
