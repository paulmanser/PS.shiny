Design section of the Regression 2 dialog box

Investigator chooses treatment levels?

Select yes if the independent variables of the linear regressions will take distinct values that are known in advance of the conduct of the study. In this case the program will prompt you for the number of levels and the values of these levels.

Select no if the independent variables are observed characteristics of the patient (e.g. body weight). You will have to estimate the standard deviation of this independent variable for both the experimental and control treatments. For normally distributed data 95% of a sample will lie within plus or minus 2 standard deviations of the mean.

Example: You might wish to assess some response of patients receiving either 50, 100, or 200 mg of treatment drug per day. In this case you must tell the program that there will be 3 treatment levels whose values are 50, 100, and 200 respectively. You will also need to state whether equal numbers of patients will receive each treatment level or specify the proportion of patients who will receive each level.

How is σ obtained?

The parameters σ, λ1, σx1, σy1 and ρ1 are all interrelated (see Dupont and Plummer 1998). You may estimate σ directly. However, you may also estimate σ indirectly by entering estimates of one of the following sets of parameters from pilot data.

σx1, σy1 and λ1

σx1, ρ1 and λ1

It is important that the preceding all come from the same pilot data. The values of σ, λ1, σx1, σy1 and ρ1 used to estimate σ need not be the same as the values of these statistics for your planned study.

λ1 is the slope estimate in the control group.

σx1 is the standard deviation of the independent variable for the control subjects.

σy1 is the standard deviation of the dependent variable in the control group.

ρ1 is the correlation coefficient between the dependent and independent variables for the control subjects.

What do you want to compare?

Select

Slopes if you wish to compare the slopes of the regression lines or

Intercepts if you wish to compare the values of the intercepts of the regression lines with their respective dependent (y) axes.
